My Background

From the serene Swiss mountains to the colorful traditions of Guatemala, my upbringing wove together a diverse blend of cultures, values and ambitions, shaping both my personal and professional journey.

Raised by a dad who could speak binary if you asked nicely, and a mom who fought battles in every room like it was her daily workout, my path was written in the stars before I even stepped onto the scene.

My academic interests led me to study at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology Lausanne (EPFL), where I mastered the fine art of thriving on a potent mix of caffeine and adrenaline-fueled deadlines, while securing a Bachelor's degree in Communication Systems and a Master's in Data Science.

My Professional Journey

Rather than embracing the allure of distant shores, my summer breaks as a student were dedicated to mastering the ebb and flow of volatile energy prices. Three consecutive summer internships at Koch Industries, allowed me to dive headfirst into the wild world of quantitative analysis and data science tools applied to energy commodity trading.

Nonetheless, I yearned for a more innovative path. The allure of doing something unique and pioneering motivated me to launch a cannabis startup in Switzerland. I immersed myself in Switzerland's entrepreneurial ecosystem, expanding my network and familiarizing myself with the country's innovation landscape. Starting a business here proved to be full of twists and turns, much like navigating a mountain pass, but the exhilarating highs (no pun intended) undoubtedly made the journey worthwhile.

Eager to maintain my entrepreneurial momentum, I became part of Capgemini's latest initiative: the data squad. I envisioned myself embarking on an adventure akin to a digital James Bond mission. However, I soon realized that our "super cool" project was essentially becoming the janitors of the digital world, tasked with tidying up the chaotic data messes of mega-corporations in legacy software systems.

Craving a break from my role as the go-to tech minion for the corporate overlords, I wanted a taste of the business and strategy world. That's when I stumbled into OWT, a place where Switzerland's finest entrepreneurs sharpened their pencils (and minds). At OWT, I felt like I was living a dream, crafting exquisite products from mere thoughts to tangible triumphs. I threw myself into the work with the passion of a love-struck teenager. But like all whirlwind romances, I eventually hit the burnout wall. Torn to bits as if I had broken up with my highschool sweetheart, I left everything to travel around the world.

Why Freelancing

After a break from the complex landscapes of large corporations and institutions, I transitioned into freelancing with enthusiasm. Embarking on this journey became necessary to embrace the freedom of achieving a harmonious balance across various facets of my life. I found in freelancing not just unmatched flexibility but a profound opportunity to tackle diverse projects around the globe, each presenting its distinct objectives, resonating deeply with my personal identity and life's mission.

My data freelancing journey has allowed me to blend professional flexibility with personal development and cultural exchange. Embracing diverse global projects enriches both my professional expertise and personal growth, creating a dynamic interplay between the work I do and the life I lead.

But my professional aspirations are just one facet of my freelancing journey. Equally important is the freedom acquired to nurture other areas of my life, such as practicing yoga and exploring music. These are not mere hobbies but essential components of my well-being and personal growth, enabling me to evolve in a more rounded and holistic manner.

Moreover, my journey is deeply rooted in a commitment to fostering a sustainable future for our society and planet.
